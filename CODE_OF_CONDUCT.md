# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Construct is a project built on the belief that software should serve people,
not the other way around. That principle extends to the community around the project.

We are committed to making participation in The Construct a welcoming experience
for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected behaviour

- Communicate with respect and good faith
- Accept constructive criticism and give it in kind
- Focus on what is best for the project and its users
- Acknowledge that people have different backgrounds and experience levels
- Give credit where it is due

### Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal attacks or insults
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Dismissing or belittling contributions based on the contributor's identity
- Sustained disruptive behaviour in discussions, issues, or pull requests
- Any conduct that would be reasonably considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests,
discussions, the project chat, and any other space where community members
interact in relation to The Construct.

It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the maintainers at
**jordan@thefreshpass.com**. All reports will be reviewed and investigated
promptly and fairly. The identity of reporters will be kept confidential.

Maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and safety of anyone who
reports an incident.

### Consequences

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

1. **Correction** — a private written warning with clarity on why the
   behaviour was unacceptable
2. **Warning** — a formal warning with conditions for continued participation
3. **Temporary ban** — temporary removal from project spaces
4. **Permanent ban** — permanent removal from all project spaces
